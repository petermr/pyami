The public is largely unfamiliar with carbon capture, utilization, and storage technologies (Tcvetkov et 30
al. 2019; L’Orange Seigo et al. 2014) (high confidence), and many people may not have formed stable 31
attitudes and risk perceptions regarding these technologies (Daamen et al. 2006; Jones et al. 2015; Van 32
Heek et al. 2017) (medium confidence). In general, low support has been reported for CCS technologies 33
(Allen and Chatterton 2013; Demski et al. 2017). When presented with neutral information on CCS, 34
people favour other mitigation options such as renewable energy and energy efficiency (de Best-35
Waldhober et al. 2009; Scheer et al. 2013; Karlstrøm and Ryghaug 2014). Although few totally reject 36
CCS, specific CCS projects have faced strong local resistance, which has contributed to the cancellation 37
