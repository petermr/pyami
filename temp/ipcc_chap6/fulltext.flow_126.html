Estimates of long run mitigation costs are highly uncertain and depend on various factors. Both faster 19
technological developments and international cooperation are consistently found to improve economic 20
outcomes (Paroussos et al. 2019). Long-term mitigation is likely to be more challenging than near-term 21
mitigation because low-cost opportunities get utilized first and efforts after that would require 22
mitigation in more challenging sectors (Section 6.6). Advances in low-carbon energy resources and 23
carriers such as next-generation biofuels, hydrogen produced from electrolysis, synthetic fuels, and 24
carbon-neutral ammonia would substantially improve the economics of net zero energy systems (high 25
confidence). Current estimates of cumulative mitigation costs are comparably high for developing 26
countries, amounting to up to 2-3% of GDP, indicating difficulties for mitigation without adequate 27
support from developed countries (Fujimori et al. 2020; Dorband et al. 2019). In scenarios involving 28
large amounts of stranded assets, the overall costs of low-carbon transitions also include the additional 29
costs of early retirements (Box 6.11). 30
Focusing only on aggregate economic outcomes neglects distributional impacts, impacts on broader 31
SDGs, and other outcomes of broad societal importance. Strategies to increase energy efficiency and 32
energy conservation are, in most instances, mutually reinforcing with strategies to support sustainable 33
development. Improving efficiency and energy conservation will promote sustainable consumption and 34
production of energy and associated materials (SDG-12) (high confidence). Contrastingly, successful 35
implementation of demand-side options requires sustainable partnerships (SDG-17) between different 36
actors in energy systems, for example governments, utilities, distributors, and consumers. Many authors 37
have argued that energy efficiency has a large untapped potential in both supply and demand (Lovins 38
2018; Méjean et al. 2019). For example, improved fossil power plant efficiency has been estimated to 39
