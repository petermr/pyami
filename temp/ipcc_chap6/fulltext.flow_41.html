6.4.2.6 Bioenergy 19
Bioenergy has the potential to be a high-value and large-scale mitigation option to support many 20
different parts of the energy system. Bioenergy could be particularly valuable for sectors with limited 21
alternatives to fossil fuels (e.g., aviation, heavy industry), production of chemicals and products, and, 22
potentially, in carbon dioxide removal (CDR) via BECCS or biochar. While traditional biomass and 23
first-generation biofuels are widely used today, the technology for large-scale production from 24
advanced processes is not competitive, and growing dedicated bioenergy crops raises a broad set of 25
sustainability concerns. Its long-term role in low-carbon energy systems is therefore uncertain (high 26
confidence). [Note that this section focuses on the key technological developments for deployment of 27
commercial bioenergy.] 28
