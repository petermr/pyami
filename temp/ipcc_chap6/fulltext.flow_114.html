6.7.3
Energy System Lock-In and Path Dependence
29
Path dependence refers to resistance to change due to favourable socio-economic conditions with 30
existing systems; decisions made in the past unduly shape future trajectories. Carbon lock-in is a 31
specific type of path dependence (Seto et al. 2016). Given that energy system mitigation will require a 32
major course change from recent history, lock-in is an important issue for emission reductions in the 33
energy sector. While lock-in is typically expressed in terms of physical infrastructure that would need 34
to be retired early to reach mitigation goals, it involves a much broader set of issues that go beyond 35
physical systems and into societal and institutional systems (Table 6.11). 36
