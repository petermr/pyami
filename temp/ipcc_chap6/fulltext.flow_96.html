6.6.3
The Institutional and Societal Characteristics of Net-zero Energy Systems
6
The transition to net zero energy systems is not just technological; it requires shifts in institutions, 7
organizations, and society more generally. As such, it involves institutional changes alongside changes 8
in supply, technology, or markets (Andrews-Speed 2016, Pai et al. 2021). Institutional relationships 9
between governments and energy sector actors (e.g., consumers, electricity companies) affect the nature 10
of net zero systems, as these entities may collaborate on or dispute net zero goals and measures to 11
achieve them. For example, following the Fukushima disaster, Japan placed emphasis on government-12
utility-public cooperation on use of nuclear power as a means of reducing carbon emissions (Sklarew 13
2018). Institutions are instrumental in shaping net zero energy systems in multiple ways, complemented 14
by and interacting with the behaviours of actors and policy regimes in these systems (Figure 6.24). 15
