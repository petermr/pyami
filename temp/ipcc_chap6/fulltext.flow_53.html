6.4.3.2
Role of digitalization and advanced control systems 1
A digitalized energy system can significantly reduce energy infrastructure investments while enhancing 2
supply security and resilience (high confidence) (Andoni et al. 2019; Strbac et al. 2020). Significant 3
progress has been made in the development of technologies essential for the transition to a digitalized 4
energy control paradigm, although the full implementation is still under development. Electrification 5
and the increased integration of the electricity system with other systems will fundamentally transform 6
the operational and planning paradigm of future energy infrastructure. A fully intelligent and 7
sophisticated coordination of the multiple systems through smart control will support this paradigm 8
shift. This shift will provide significant savings through better utilization of existing infrastructure 9
locally, regionally, nationally, and internationally. Supply system reliability will be enhanced through 10
advanced control of local infrastructure (Strbac et al. 2015a). Furthermore, this paradigm shift offers 11
the potential to increase energy efficiency through a combination of technologies that gather and analyse 12
data and consequently optimize energy use in real-time. 13
The transition to advanced data-driven control of energy system operations (Sun et al. 2019a; Cremer 14
et al. 2019) will require advanced information and communication technologies and infrastructure, 15
including the internet, wireless networks, computers, software, middleware, smart sensors, internet of 16
things components, and dedicated technological developments (Hossein Motlagh et al. 2020). The 17
transition will raise standardization and cybersecurity issues, given that digitalization can become a 18
single point of failure for the complete system (Unsal et al. 2021; Ustun and Hussain 2019). 19
Implementing peer-to-peer energy trading based on blockchain is expected to be one of the key elements 20
of next-generation electricity systems (Qiu et al. 2021). This trading will enable consumers to drive 21
system operation and future design, increasing overall system efficiency and security of supply while 22
reducing emissions without sacrificing users’ privacy (Andoni et al. 2019; Ahl et al. 2020). When 23
deployed with smart contracts, this concept will be suitable for energy systems involving many 24
participants, where a prerequisite is digitalization (e.g., smart meters, end-use demand control systems) 25
(Teufel et al. 2019; Juhar and Khaled 2018). 26
6.4.3.3
System benefits of flexibility technologies and advanced control systems 27
New sources of flexibility and advanced control systems provide a significant opportunity to reduce 28
low-carbon energy system costs by enhancing operating efficiency and reducing energy infrastructure 29
and low-carbon generation investments, while continuing to meet security requirements (high 30
confidence). In the U.S, for example, one study found that flexibility in buildings alone could reduce 31

Efficient energy system operation: Flexibility technologies such as storage, demand-side response, 34

balancing. This balancing has historically been provided by conventional fossil-fuel generation 36
(Nuytten et al. 2013). 37
•
Savings in investment in low carbon/renewable generation capacity: System flexibility sources can 38
absorb or export surplus electricity, thus reducing or avoiding energy curtailment and reducing the 39
need for firm low-carbon capacity such as nuclear and fossil-fuel plants with CCS (Newbery et al. 40
2013; Solomon et al. 2019). For example, one study found that flexibility technologies and 41
advanced control systems could reduce the need for nuclear power by 14 GW and offshore wind by 42
20 GW in the UK’s low-carbon transition (Strbac et al. 2015b). 43
•
Reduced need for backup capacity: System flexibility can reduce energy demand peaks, reducing 44
the required generation capacity to maintain the security of supply, producing significant savings 45
in generation investments (Strbac et al. 2020). 46
