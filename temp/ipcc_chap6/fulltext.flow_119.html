Fossil fuels in a low-carbon transition
1
Global fossil fuel use will need to decline substantially by 2050 to limit likely warming to 2°C or below, 2
and it must decline substantially by 2030 to limit warming to 1.5°C with no or limited overshoot (high 3
confidence). Failing to reduce global fossil fuel use below today’s levels by 2030 will make it more 4
challenging to limit likely warming to below 2°C. (high confidence). Fossil fuel use declines by 260-5
330 EJ (52-73% from 2020 levels, interquartile range) through 2050 to limit warming to 1.5°C with no 6
or limited overshoot and 124-231 EJ (23-51% reduction compared to 2020 levels) to limit likely 7
warming to 2°C; this will require a significant reduction in coal, oil and gas investments. Fossil fuels 8
account for about 80% of primary energy today. In scenarios limiting warming to 1.5°C with limited or 9
no overshoot, fossil energy provides 59-69% (interquartile range) primary energy in 2030 and 25-40s% 10
primary energy in 2050 (AR6 database). In scenarios limiting likely warming to 2°C or below, fossil 11
energy provides 71-75% (interquartile range) primary energy in 2030 and 41-57% primary energy in 12
2050 (AR6 database). The timeline for reducing production and usage varies across coal, oil, and gas 13
due to their differing carbon intensities and their differing uses. 14
