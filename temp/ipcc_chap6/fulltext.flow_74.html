The conclusions regarding climate change impacts on hydropower vary due to differences in modelling 9
assumptions and methodology, such as choice of the climate and hydrological models, choice of metrics 10
(e.g., projected production vs hydropower potential), level of modelling details between local and global 11
studies, reservoir operation assumptions. Also important is how hydropower production matches up 12
with other reservoir purposes, accounting for other water and energy users, and how the competing uses 13
are impacted by climate change (Turner et al. 2017; van Vliet et al. 2016b). Nonetheless, analyses 14
consistently demonstrate that the global impact of climate change on hydropower will be small, but the 15
regional impacts will be larger, and will be both positive and negative (Figure 6.20) Gross global 16
hydropower potential in the 2050s has been estimated to slightly decrease (Hamududu and Killingtveit 17
2012) between 0.4% (for the low emission scenario) and 6.1% (for the highest emission scenario) for 18
the 2080s compared to 1971–2000 (van Vliet et al. 2016a). 19
Regional changes in hydropower are estimated from 5–20% increases for most areas in high latitudes 20
(van Vliet et al. 2016b; Turner et al. 2017) to decreases of 5–20% in areas with increased drought 21
conditions (Cronin et al. 2018). Models show a consistent increase in streamflow and hydropower 22
production by 2080 in high latitudes of the northern hemisphere and parts of the tropics (Figure 6.20) 23
(e.g., central Africa and southern Asia) while decreasing in the U.S., southern and central Europe, 24
Southeast Asia and southern South America, Africa and Australia (van Vliet et al. 2016c,a). Decreases 25
in hydropower production are indicated for parts of North America, central and southern Europe, the 26
Middle East, central Asia and Southern South America. Studies disagree on the changes in hydropower 27
production in China, central South America, and partially in southern Africa (Solaun and Cerdá 2019; 28
Hamududu and Killingtveit 2012; van Vliet et al. 2016b; Fan et al. 2020). 29
