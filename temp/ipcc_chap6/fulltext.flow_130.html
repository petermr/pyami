FAQ 6.3. What are the most important steps to decarbonize the energy system? 15
To create a low-carbon energy system, emissions must be reduced across all parts of the system, and 16
not just one or two. This means, for example, reducing the emissions from producing electricity, driving 17
cars, hauling freight, heating and cooling buildings, powering data centers, and manufacturing goods. 18
There are more opportunities to reduce emissions over the next decade in some sectors compared to 19
others. For example, it’s possible to substantially reduce electricity emissions over the next decade by 20
investing in low-carbon electricity sources, while at the same time halting the construction of new coal-21
fired power plants, retiring existing coal-fired power plants or retrofitting them with CCS, and limiting 22
the construction of new gas-fired power plants. There are also opportunities to increase the number of 23
electric cars, trucks, and other vehicles on the road, or to use electricity rather than natural gas or coal 24
to heat homes. And across the whole energy system, emissions can be reduced by using more efficient 25
technologies. While these and other actions will be critical over the coming decade, it is also important 26
to remember that the low-carbon energy transition needs to extend for many decades into the future to 27
limit warming. This means that it is important now to improve and test out options that could be useful 28
later on, for example, producing hydrogen from low-carbon sources or producing bioenergy from crops 29
that require less land than those of today. 30
