6.4.4
Energy Storage for Low-Carbon Grids
5
Energy storage technologies make low carbon electricity systems more cost-effective, allowing VRE 6
technologies to replace more expensive firm low carbon generation technologies (Carbon Trust 2016) 7
and reducing investment costs in backup generation, interconnection, transmission, and distribution 8
network upgrades (high confidence). Energy system decarbonization relies on increased electrification 9
(Section 6.6.2.3.). Meeting increasing demands with variable renewable sources presents challenges 10
and could lead to costly infrastructure reinforcements. Energy storage enables electricity from variable 11
renewables to be matched against evolving demands across both time and space, using short-, medium- 12
and long-term storage of excess energy for delivery later or different location. In 2017, an estimated 13
4.67 TWh (0.017 EJ) of electricity storage was in operation globally (IRENA 2017). If the integration 14
of renewables is doubled from 2014 levels by 2030, the total capacity of global electricity storage could 15
triple, reaching 11.89–15.27 TWh (0.043–0.055 EJ)(IRENA 2017b). 16
