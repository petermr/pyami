A critical challenge in the assessment of energy sector progress is how to measure societal, institutional, 1
and political progress. These factors are difficult to quantify, yet they are fundamental determinants of 2
the ability to reduce emissions. Public opinion, special interest politics, implications of mitigation for 3
employment, energy subsidies, and energy policies are all critical indicators of progress. In addition, 4
while much of the literature focuses on national level action, mitigation is increasingly being led by 5
cities, states, provinces, businesses, and other subnational or non-national actors. Understanding the 6
progress of these actors will be critical to assess energy system mitigation progress. New research is 7
needed to better assess these “societal” indicators and the role of non-national actors. 8
