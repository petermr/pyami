6.6.2
Configurations of Net-zero Energy Systems
8
Net-zero energy systems entail trade-offs across economic, environmental, and social dimensions 9
(Davis et al. 2018). Many socioeconomic, policy, and market uncertainties will also influence the 10
configuration of net zero energy systems (van Vuuren et al. 2018; Krey et al. 2019; Bistline et al. 2019; 11
Smith et al. 2015, Azevedo et al. 2021, Pye et al, 2021). There are reasons that countries might focus 12
on one system configuration versus another, including cost, resource endowments, related industrial 13
bases, existing infrastructure, geography, governance, public acceptance, and other policy priorities 14
(Section 6.6.4 and Chapter 18 of WGII). 15
Explorations of net zero energy systems have been emerging in the detailed systems modelling literature 16
(Azevedo et al. 2021; Bistline 2021b). Reports associated with net zero economy-wide targets for 17
countries and subnational entities typically do not provide detailed roadmaps or modelling but discuss 18
high-level guiding principles, though more detailed studies are emerging at national levels (Williams et 19
al. 2021a; Duan et al. 2021; Capros et al. 2019; Wei et al. 2020). Most analysis has focused on 20
identifying potential decarbonization technologies and pathways for different sectors, enumerating 21
opportunities and barriers for each, their costs, highlighting robust insights, and characterizing key 22
uncertainties (Hepburn et al. 2019; Davis et al. 2018). 23

in broad strokes (AR6 database), offering internally consistent global scenarios to link global warming 26
