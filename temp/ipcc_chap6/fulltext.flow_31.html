All major onshore wind markets have experienced rapid growth in both rotor diameter (from 81.2 m in 2
2010 to 120 m in 2020) (IRENA 2021b), and average power ratings (from 1.9 MW in 2010 to 3 MW 3
in 2020). The generation capacity of offshore wind turbines grew by a factor of 3.7 in less than two 4
decades, from 1.6 MW in 2000 to 6 MW in 2020 (Wiser et al. 2021). Floating foundations could 5
revolutionize offshore wind power by tapping into the abundant wind potential in deeper waters. This 6
technology is particularly important for regions where coastal waters are too deep for fixed-bottom 7
wind turbines. Floating wind farms potentially offer economic and environmental benefits compared 8
with fixed-bottom designs due to less-invasive activity on the seabed during installation, but the long-9
term ecological effects are unknown and meteorological conditions further offshore and in deeper 10
waters are harsher on wind turbine components (IRENA 2019c). A radical new class of wind energy 11
converters has also been conceived under the name of Airborne Wind Energy Systems that can harvest 12
strong, high-altitude winds (typically between 200–800m), which are inaccessible by traditional wind 13
turbines (Cherubini et al. 2015). This technology has seen development and testing of small devices 14
(Watson et al. 2019). 15
Wind capacity factors have increased over the last decade (Figure 6.11). The capacity factor for onshore 16
wind farms increased from 27% in 2010 to 36% in 2020 (IRENA 2021a). The global average offshore 17
capacity factor has decreased from a peak of 45% in 2017. This has been driven by the increased share 18
of offshore development in China, where projects are often near-shore and use smaller wind turbines 19
than in Europe (IRENA 2021b). Improvements in capacity factors also come from increased 20
functionality of wind turbines and wind farms. Manufactures can adapt the wind turbine generator to 21
the wind conditions. Turbines for windy sites have smaller generators and smaller specific capacity per 22
rotor area, and therefore operate more efficiently and reach full capacity for a longer time period (Rohrig 23
et al. 2019). 24
