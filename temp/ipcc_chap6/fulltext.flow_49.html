Waste-to-Energy 39
Waste-to-energy (WTE) is a strategy to recover energy from waste in a form of consumable heat, 40
electricity, or fuel (Zhao et al. 2016). Thermal (incineration, gasification, and pyrolysis) and biological 41
(anaerobic digestion and landfill gas to energy) technologies are commonly used (Ahmad et al. 42
2020).When WTE technologies are equipped with proper air pollution reduction facilities they can 43
contribute to clean electricity production and reduction of GHG emissions. However, if not properly 44
operated, they can exacerbate air quality issues. 45
