Energy System Integration
22
Greenhouse gases are emitted across all economic activities. Therefore, cost-effective decarbonization 23
requires a “system of systems” approach that considers the interaction between different energy sectors 24
and systems. Flexibility technologies and advanced control of integrated energy systems (e.g., 25
considering the interaction between electricity, heating/cooling, gas/hydrogen, transport sectors) could 26
reduce energy infrastructure investments substantially in future low-carbon energy systems (Strbac et 27
al. 2015b; Jacobson et al. 2019) 28
The electricity grid will serve as a backbone of future low-carbon energy systems. Integration of large 29
amounts of VRE generation (Hansen et al. 2019), particularly wind and solar generation (Perez et al. 30
2019; Bistline and Young 2019), presents economic and technical challenges to electricity system 31
management across different timescales from sub-seconds, hours, days, seasons, to multiple years. 32
Furthermore, electrification of segments of the transport and heat sectors could disproportionately 33
increase peak demand relative to supply (Bistline et al. 2021). Increases in peak demand may require 34
reinforcing network infrastructures and generation in the historical passive system operation paradigm 35
(Strbac et al. 2020). 36
These challenges to electricity system management can be addressed through system integration and a 37
digitalized control paradigm involving advanced information and communication technologies. Real-38
time maintenance of supply-demand balance and sufficient flexibility technologies such as electricity 39
storage, flexible demand, and grid forming converters (Strbac et al. 2015a; López Prol and Schill 2021) 40
would be increasingly valuable for incorporating larger amounts of VRE generation. This flexibility 41
will be particularly important to deal with sudden losses of supply, for example, due to a failure of a 42
large generator or interconnector or a rapid increase in demand (Teng et al. 2017; Chamorro et al. 2020). 43
