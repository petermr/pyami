Advanced pump-turbines are being developed, allowing both reversible and variable-speed operation, 3
supporting frequency control and grid stability with improved round-trip efficiencies (Ardizzon et al. 4
2014). New possibilities are being explored for small-scale PHS installations and expanding the 5
potential for siting (Kougias et al. 2019). For example, in underwater PHS, the upper reservoir is the 6
sea, and the lower is a hollow deposit at the seabed. Seawater is pumped out of the deposit to store off-7
peak energy and re-enters through turbines to recharge it (Kougias et al. 2019). Using a similar concept, 8
underground siting in abandoned mines and caverns could be developed reasonably quickly (IEA 9
2020h). Storage of energy as gravitational potential can also be implemented using materials other than 10
water, such as rocks and sand. Pumped technology is a mature technology (Barbour et al. 2016; Rehman 11
et al. 2015) and can be important in supporting the transition to future low carbon electricity grids (IHA 12
2021). 13
Batteries. There are many types of batteries, all having unique features and suitability (c), but their key 14
feature is their rapid response times. A rechargeable battery cell is charged by using electricity to drive 15
ions from one electrode to another, with the reverse occurring on discharge, producing a usable electric 16
current (Crabtree et al. 2015). While lead-acid batteries (LABs) have been widely used for automotive 17
and grid applications for decades (May et al. 2018), li-ion batteries (LIBs) are increasingly being used 18
in grid-scale projects (Crabtree et al. 2015), displacing LABs. The rapid response time of batteries 19
makes them suitable for enhanced frequency regulation and voltage support, enabling the integration of 20
variable renewables into electricity grids (Strbac and Aunedi 2016). Batteries can provide almost all 21
electricity services, except for seasonal storage. Lithium-ion batteries, in particular, can store energy 22
and power in small volumes and with low weight, making them the default choice for EVs (Placke et 23
al. 2017). EV batteries are expected to form a distributed storage resource as this market grows, both 24
impacting and supporting the grid (Staffell and Rustomji 2016). 25
