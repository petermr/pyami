Like PV, CSP facilities can deliver large amounts of power (up to 200 MW per unit) and maintain 7
substantial thermal storage, which is valuable for load balancing over the diurnal cycle (McPherson et 8
al. 2020). However, unlike PV, CSP can only use direct sunlight, constraining its cost-effectiveness to 9
North Africa, the Middle East, Southern Africa, Australia, the Western U.S., parts of South America 10
(Peru, Chile), the Western part of China, and Australia (Deng et al. 2015; Dupont et al. 2020). Parabolic 11
troughs, central towers and parabolic dishes are the three leading solar thermal technologies (Wang et 12
al. 2017d). Parabolic troughs represented approximately 70% of new capacity in 2018 with the balance 13
made up by central tower plants (Islam et al. 2018). Especially promising research directions are on 14
tower-based designs that can achieve high temperatures, useful for industrial heat and energy storage 15
(Mehos et al. 2017), and direct steam generation designs (Islam et al. 2018). Costs of CSP have fallen 16
by nearly half since AR5 (Figure 6.8) albeit at a slower rate than PV. Since AR5, almost all new CSP 17
plants have storage (Figure 6.9)(Thonig 2020). 18

Solar energy elicits favourable public responses in most countries (high confidence) (Bessette and Arvai 24
2018; Hanger et al. 2016; Jobin and Siegrist 2018; Ma et al. 2015; Mcgowan and Sauter 2005; Hazboun 25
and Boudet 2020; Roddis et al. 2019). Solar energy is perceived as clean and environmentally friendly 26
with few downsides (Faiers and Neame 2006; Whitmarsh et al. 2011b). Key motivations for 27
homeowners to adopt photovoltaic systems are expected financial gains, environmental benefits, the 28
desire to become more self-sufficient, and peer expectations (Korcaj et al. 2015; Palm 2017; Vasseur 29
and Kemp 2015). Hence, the observability of photovoltaic systems can facilitate adoption (Boudet 30
2019). The main barriers to the adoption of solar PV by households are its high upfront costs, aesthetics, 31
landlord-tenant incentives, and concerns about performance and reliability (Whitmarsh et al. 2011b; 32
Vasseur and Kemp 2015; Faiers and Neame 2006). 33
