Within this context, this chapter has two main objectives. First, it aims to assess specific, individual 8
mitigation options in energy supply, energy transformation, and energy transportation and transmission. 9
This assessment is complementary to a set of chapters that explore mitigation options in agriculture, 10
forestry, and other land uses (Chapter 7), urban systems and other settlements (Chapter 8), buildings 11
(Chapter 9), transport (Chapter 10), industry (Chapter 11), and cross-sectoral perspectives (Chapter 12). 12
Second, this chapter aims to assess system-level mitigation opportunities and challenges across the 13
entirety of energy systems. These systems include energy supply, transformation, transmission, storage, 14
transportation, and end-uses. They also include the societal systems that interact with the physical 15
energy system. As energy systems become increasingly integrated and interconnected, a system-wide 16
perspective is necessary for understanding mitigation opportunities and challenges. 17
Within this context, this chapter addresses six topics, each of which is addressed in a separate section. 18
First, Section 6.2 defines the scope of the energy system. Section 6.3 then discusses the recent trends in 19
energy systems that might exert the most significant influence on energy system evolution and options 20
for reducing emissions. Section 6.4 assesses the status and potential of individual energy supply, 21
transformation, storage, transportation and transmission, and integration mitigation options in the 22
energy sector. Section 6.5 explores how climate change might affect energy systems and alter potential 23
energy system mitigation options and strategies. Section 6.6 identifies key characteristics of net zero 24
energy systems – those that emit very little or no CO
2
. Section 6.7 explores transition pathways toward 25
and through net zero energy systems. Across all of these sections, the chapter aims to explore the ways 26
that energy sector mitigation options and strategies interact with SDGs and other societal and 27
environmental goals. 28
