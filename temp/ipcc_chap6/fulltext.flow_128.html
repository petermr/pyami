Electrification also improves energy efficiency, with corresponding implications for development goals 4
For example, the availability of electric cooking may reduce the cooking primary energy requirement 5
considerably compared to traditional stoves (Batchelor et al. 2019; Yang and Yang 2018; Khan and 6
Alam 2020) while also promoting improved indoor air quality (SDG-3). Similarly, PV-powered 7
irrigation and water pumping reduces pumping energy demands, which has the added advantage of 8
promoting SDG-6 on clean water (Elkadeem et al. 2019; Rathore et al. 2018). 9
Phasing out fossil fuels in favour of low-carbon sources, is likely to have considerable SDG benefits, 10
particularly if tradeoffs such as unemployment to fossil fuel workers are minimized (high confidence). 11
A phaseout of coal (Box 6.2, Section 6.3) will support SDGs 3, 7 and 14, but it is also anticipated to 12
create large job losses if not properly managed. At the same time, there are large potential employment 13
opportunities that may be created in alternative sectors such as renewables and bioenergy for both 14
skilled and unskilled workers. “Sustainable transition” pathways have indicated a complete fossil 15
phaseout which could entail numerous other co-benefits. For instance, fossil fuels are estimated to 16
generate only 2.65 jobs per USD 1M as compared to projected 7.49 from renewables (Garrett-Peltier 17
2017). Similar synergies may also emerge for nuclear power in the long-term though the high costs 18
create tradeoffs in developing country contexts (Castor et al. 2020; Agyekum et al. 2020). While 19
bioenergy production may create jobs, it may also be problematic for SDG-2 on zero hunger by affecting 20
the supplies and prices of food. Phasing out of fossil fuels will also improve air quality (SDG-3) and 21
premature deaths by reducing PM2.5 emissions, (He et al. 2020; Li et al. 2020c). Energy transitions 22
from fossil fuels to renewables, as well as within fossil fuels (coal to gas switching), are already 23
occurring in some regions, spurred by climate concerns, health concerns, market dynamics, or consumer 24
choice (for example in the transport sector).
