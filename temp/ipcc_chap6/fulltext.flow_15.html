Non-climate factors continue to drive energy systems changes 5
While energy system changes are vital to climate mitigation, recent energy system changes have arisen 6
in response to a much broader set of factors. Important factors include economic growth, energy access, 7
energy justice, energy security, air pollution, technological progress in low-emissions technologies, 8
local job creation. Several of these are discussed here. 9
Energy Access. Between 2000 and 2019, the proportion of the population with access to electricity 10
increased from 73% to 90% (IEA 2020c). Although most of those people gaining access to energy have 11
gained access to fossil fuel-based electricity, an increasing number are gaining access to electricity from 12
renewable sources. Low-emissions, decentralised systems are proving a cost-effective way to provide 13
electricity in rural areas (Scott et al. 2016; IEA 2019b; Muchunku et al. 2018), although the use of diesel 14
generators continues in some remote areas. Between 2000 and 2019 the proportion of the population 15
with access to clean cooking (modern fuels and/or improved biomass cookstoves) rose from 52% to 16
66%. 17
Energy Security. The ability of countries to maintain access to reliable and affordable energy resources 18
continues to shape energy policy. Energy security is perceived as a national security issue and often 19
prioritized over climate concerns (Nyman 2018). The linkage between climate and energy security is 20
now widely recognized (Toke and Vezirgiannidou 2013, Fu et al. 2021; La Viña et al. 2018; Blarke and 21
Lund 2007; World Energy Council 2020; United Nations 2021). Approaches to energy security are 22
frequently driven by the scope of domestic energy resources. For example, energy security concerns 23
have led to continued reliance on domestic coal production and consumption (Jakob et al. 2020) and 24
increased investment in domestic renewable generation (Konstantinos & Ioannidis, 2017). LNG 25
Importers have diversified their sources as reliance on LNG has increased (Vivoda 2019). 26
