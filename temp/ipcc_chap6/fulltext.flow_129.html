Low-carbon energy systems will be similar to those of today in that they will provide many of the same 19
services as today – for example, heating and cooling homes, travelling to work or on vacation, 20
transporting goods and services, and powering manufacturing. But future energy systems may be 21
different in that people may also demand new services that aren’t foreseen today, just as people now 22
use energy for many information technology uses that were not anticipated 50 years ago. More 23
importantly, low-carbon energy systems will be different in the way that energy is produced, 24
transformed, and used to provide these services. In the future, almost all electricity will be produced 25
from sources that emit little or no CO
2
, such as solar power, wind power, nuclear power, bioenergy, 26
hydropower, geothermal power, or fossil energy in which the CO
2
is captured and stored. Electricity, 27
hydrogen, and bioenergy will be used in many situations where fossil fuels are used today, for example, 28
in cars or heating homes. And energy is likely to be used more efficiently than today, for example, 29
through more efficient cars, trucks, and appliances, buildings that use very little energy, and greater use 30
or public transportation. All of these changes may require new policies, institutions, and even new ways 31
for people to live their lives. And fundamental to all of these changes is that low-carbon energy systems 32
will use far less fossil fuel than today. 33
