Oil and coal consistently rank among the least preferred energy sources in many countries (high 20
confidence). The main perceived advantage of fossil energy is the relatively low costs, and emphasizing 21
these costs might increase acceptability somewhat (Pohjolainen et al. 2018; Hazboun and Boudet 2020; 22
Boyd et al. 2019). Acceptability of fossil fuels is on average similar to acceptability of nuclear energy, 23
although evaluations are less polarized. People evaluate natural gas as somewhat more acceptable than 24
other fossil fuels, although they generally oppose hydraulic fracturing (Clarke et al. 2016). Yet, natural 25
gas is evaluated as less acceptable than renewable energy sources, although evaluations of natural gas 26
and biogas are similar (Liebe and Dobers 2019; Plum et al. 2019). Acceptability of fossil energy tends 27
to be higher in countries and regions that strongly rely on them for their energy production (Boyd et al. 28
2019; Pohjolainen et al. 2018). Combining fossil fuels with CCS can increase their acceptability (Van 29
Rijnsoever et al. 2015; Bessette and Arvai 2018). Some people seem ambivalent about natural gas, as 30
they perceive both benefits (e.g., affordability, less carbon emissions than coal) and disadvantages (e.g., 31
finite resource, contributing to climate change) (Blumer et al. 2018). 32

Geothermal energy is heat stored in the Earth’s subsurface and is a renewable resource that can be 40
sustainably exploited. The geophysical potential of geothermal resources is 1.3 to 13 times the global 41

thermal applications, including space heating and industrial heat input, or converted to electricity 43
depending on the source temperature (Moya et al. 2018; REN21 2019; Limberger et al. 2018). 44
